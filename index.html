I'm Aniket Paul, an Assistant Professor based in Gujarat, India, where I teach undergraduate students cybersecurity concepts, digital forensics, and programming fundamentals. My classroom extends beyond slides — I run hands-on security labs where students get comfortable with tools and techniques that real analysts use every day.

My relationship with Linux started as curiosity and evolved into a daily practice. I troubleshoot, experiment, break things intentionally, and rebuild — because that's how I truly understand how systems behave under pressure. This philosophy shapes every course I teach.

Beyond systems, I'm deeply interested in threat intelligence, OSINT methodologies, and how open-source information can be leveraged both offensively and defensively. I follow the AI space closely and actively integrate AI-assisted workflows into research and teaching.

My approach to cybersecurity education is simple: you cannot defend what you don't understand from the attacker's perspective.

"Security is learned best when you're allowed to break things."

I structure labs so students encounter real failure modes — misconfigured services, vulnerable code, unexpected system behavior. I encourage adopting the attacker's mindset: not to cause harm, but to fully understand the attack surface. Practical learning, honest experimentation, and intellectual curiosity are the pillars of my classroom.
